## Characters

### Protagonists / The Alliance (Anti-Hero Alliance, "The Forces of Freedom")

**Marquis Deshaun Whitacre ("The Free Agent" / "Bannon" / "The Diplomat" / "The Golden Voice" / "The Architect" / "The Broker")** — The protagonist. Numerology: Life Path (LP) 3, Destiny/Expression (Exp) 5, Soul Urge (SU) 11/2. Birth date November 12, 1996. Astrology: Scorpio Sun (4th House), Leo Rising (12°), Moon in Sagittarius (5th House), Mars in Virgo (1st House), Mercury in Scorpio (4th House), MC in Taurus (6°). A charismatic, technical, high-risk performer whose public flash (LP 3/Exp 5) hides a secret need for loyalty and partnership (SU 2). Goes through a face-to-heel-to-redemption arc: rises as a beloved tag-team "Diplomat," is betrayed by his partner ("The Guardian"), transforms into the cold, controlling heel "The Architect"/"The Broker," and eventually masks himself as **Bannon, The Broken Architect** — a Mankind/Kane-and-MF-DOOM-inspired masked monster whose steel mask represents emotional self-protection. Mantra: "Vulnerability is a choice. I choose steel." Style: Agile/Technical/High-Risk (uses old Exp 5 moves and raw LP 3 promos). His finisher as Bannon is a devastating submission hold "held until the opponent is visibly broken." Must ultimately defeat Edwin J. Kennedy and resist the temptation of Cassian Thorne.

**Atlas Vance ("The Anchor")** — Protagonist's closest ally and the Alliance's "moral structure." LP 4, Exp 22/4, SU 6. Cancer Sun, Mars in Gemini. OTR Persona: "The Studious Engineer" — highly organized, meticulous, calm to the point of monotone, enjoys architectural design. Style: Strong Base/Counter-Wrestling/Submissions ("Mars in Gemini counter-offense"). His integrity must withstand the Corporate Structure's attacks; he is key to triggering Marquis's loyalty (SU 2).

**Chief Red Cloud** — LP 9/Exp 6. Moral Core Ally; provides wisdom and ethical guidance. Style: Grounding Technician/Submissions. Finisher: **The Ancestor's Lock** (Texas Cloverleaf). Independent (Ally), Native American.

**The Lion of Punjab** — LP 6/Exp 5. Trusted Loyalist whose loyalty counters Cassian Thorne's temptation. OTR Persona: "The Community Chef" — runs a small restaurant, believes communal food/loyalty are the highest service. Style: Power Brawler/Traditional Impact. Finisher: **The Unity Bomb** (Running Powerbomb). Independent (Ally), Punjabi.

**Lady Rhiannon** — LP 2/Exp 6. Global Diplomat who challenges the corporate system with charismatic diplomacy. OTR Persona: "The Political Activist" — campaigns for unionization and industry fairness. Style: Regal Showwoman/Clean Technique. Finisher: **The Peace Accord** (Figure-Four Leglock). Independent (Ally), British.

**Agent Canuck** — LP 3/Exp 9. Communicative Rebel who uses wit/agility to expose corporate hypocrisy in promos. OTR Persona: "The Stand-up Comedian." Style: Agile/Submission/Witty. Finisher: **The North Star Lock** (Flexible Figure-Four). Independent (Ally), Canadian.

**The Celtic Fury** — LP 5/Exp 9. Wildcard embodying the reckless, unbound freedom (Exp 5) Marquis is reclaiming. Style: Chaotic Brawler/High-Impact Throws. Finisher: **The Braveheart Bomb** (Delayed Brainbuster). Independent (Ally), Scottish.

**Rey "La Pluma" Fuego (The Fire Feather)** — LP 3/Exp 9. Lucha Libre Tecnico (good guy); mask adorned with flame/wing designs; embodies pure joyful LP 3 performance and Exp 5 freedom that Bannon suppressed. Signatures: El Grito del Fuego (619 into springboard cross body), La Flecha Enmascarada (elevated diving senton). Finisher: **El Beso del Sol** ("The Kiss of the Sun," Springboard 450 Splash); secondary finisher La Tijera Mortale (headscissors takedown into pin). Style: Aereo/High-Flyer/Showmanship.

**El Toro de Oro (The Golden Bull)** — LP 4/Exp 2. Masked Lucha powerhouse, intensely loyal/protective partner; contrasts Bannon's toxic alliances. Signatures: El Cuerno Dorado ("The Golden Horn," running spear), El Cierre de las Pampas (spinebuster). Finishers: **El Grito de la Bestia** ("The Cry of the Beast," sit-out powerbomb), La Estocada Final ("The Final Thrust," sharpshooter). Style: Power Lucha/Submission.

## World Rules

- **Promotions/Companies:**
  - **AWE — Apex Wrestling Entertainment** (also called "The American Wrestling Establishment" in one pass): the corporate, American-based promotion; Owner/CEO is Edwin J. Kennedy. Home promotion of Marquis/Bannon and Atlas Vance.
  - **JPCW — Japan Proresu Championship Wrestling** (also glossed "Japan Pro Wrestling/Global Network"): Stan "Honey" Combs's promotion; source of global/technical talent and the "Cyborg Project" conspiracy; described as the primary vehicle for Combs's corporate takeover of AWE.
  - **Lucha Libre circuit**: separate global talent pool (Rey Fuego, El Toro de Oro, Sombra Negra) representing "Expressive Freedom" as a foil to AWE's corporate control/structure.
  - Independent/global free agents exist outside both promotions ("Global Independents and Alliance Forces" / "Independent Variables").

- **Numerology/Astrology as a world mechanic**: Every character is explicitly built from a Numerology profile (Life Path/LP, Destiny or Expression/Exp, Soul Urge/SU) plus a Natal Chart (Sun/Moon/Rising/Mars/Mercury/MC placements). These numbers are treated as the literal "character engine" driving personality, ring style, and story arc — e.g., Marquis's "Exp 5 vs SU 2" conflict (freedom/change vs. need for loyal partnership) is the explicit thematic spine of his redemption arc. Numbers are described as "locked" once finalized, i.e., treated as canon facts, not just flavor.

- **OTR ("Outside the Ring") Persona Layer**: Every character has both an in-ring gimmick and a separate "outside the ring" civilian personality, explicitly classified as **Aligned** (ring persona matches real personality), **Contrasting** (ring persona is opposite of real personality), or **Blurred** (mixed/ambiguous). This is described as the key device for making a 41-person roster memorable.

- **Kayfabe as literal world-control mechanic**: Kennedy is described as having built "the entire structure (Kayfabe)" — kayfabe is treated in-universe as a tool of corporate/political control, not just performance convention ("the Tower of Babel that still stands").

- **Canon roster scope**: The "Definitive Master Roster" is explicitly locked at **41 characters** for Book 3, divided into AWE Core Talent, JPCW & Global Assets, and Global Independents/Alliance Forces. Multiple passes in the document re-confirm "41 characters" as a fixed, audited number — suggesting roster size itself is treated as a canon fact to preserve.

- **Four-Faction structure for Book 3**: I. The Anti-Hero Alliance ("Forces of Freedom"), II. The Corporate Structure ("Tyranny of LP 33/6," led by Kennedy & Combs), III. The Agents of Chaos & Temptation, IV. The Independent Variables (For Hire).

- **Titles/Belts mentioned**: "Tag Team Championship" (won and lost by Marquis and the Guardian), "World Champion"/"World Title" (Cain Elias is debated as the new champion; Marquis/Bannon as the Architect also wins a World Championship "through a technicality or referee mistake" during his heel run, with the title reframed as "a symbol of corporate control, not sporting achievement").

- **Bannon mask/persona rules**: The mask is explicitly framed as symbolic armor (Sun in Scorpio = privacy/protection) rather than a simple intimidation gimmick; it has a "functional jaw" attachment enabling promos. Visual reference described as a fusion of "Mankind/Kane intensity with the cryptic structure of MF DOOM." Bannon's mantra: "Vulnerability is a choice. I choose steel."

- **Story structure / booking phases (template used repeatedly)**: Wrestling arcs in this universe are explicitly plotted in a 4-phase booking template — Ascent (face run) -> Betrayal/Catalyst -> Heel Reign -> Fall/Redemption — tied directly to the numerology conflict engine.

- **Canon timeline**: Book 1 = "Off The Top Rope: Life In Limbo." Books 1 and 2 establish the Alliance's formation and the defeat of the "Agents of Chaos" (Stick-Up defeated by Marquis's logic in "Ch. 28"). Book 3 is the planned "final structural showdown" / "final systemic battle" against Kennedy and Combs, introducing Cassian Thorne as the new Book 3 temptation threat.

## Open Threads / Notes

- **Cassian Thorne's recruitment attempt**: Explicitly flagged as the central Book 3 threat to the Bannon/Atlas partnership ("synastry") — Thorne will try to convince Marquis that solitary power (LP 1) beats loyalty/partnership (SU 2). Unresolved going into Book 3.
- **Stick-Up's dormant data/legacy**: Defeated in Book 2 (Ch. 28) but explicitly noted as a loose thread — "the potential for him to be reactivated or for his data to be weaponized against the Alliance by Kennedy/Combs remains."
- **Mr. Zero Point as wildcard distraction**: Flagged as a possible "final, desperate distraction" Kennedy/Combs could deploy before the climactic systemic battle — chaos used to delay/dilute the main confrontation.
- **Marquis's Original Partner / "the Guardian"**: The source of the original betrayal that created Bannon. Text suggests "a brief, final appearance could show Marquis's growth, proving he is past the original trauma" — an explicitly unresolved/optional callback thread. Note: naming is inconsistent across documents (sometimes "the Guardian," sometimes folded into "Bannon's Former Manager" / Cody Callahan) — worth reconciling before using in game canon.
- **Cody Callahan's dual role**: Positioned as both Marquis's former manager (betrayal figure) and later a bodyguard for Kennedy/Combs — flagged as possibly returning "to manipulate the Alliance" or being used as "a disposable pawn against Marquis." Toxic Pawn status leaves him available for a future heel turn or redemption beat.
- **The Cyborg Project**: Stan Combs's conspiracy program that produced Stick-Up — described as part of a broader "global/government conspiracy" that must be "exposed and neutralized on a legal/corporate level before the final confrontation." Largely unexplored beyond Stick-Up himself — an open worldbuilding thread (who else might be a "Cyborg Project" asset?).
- **Faction fluidity of "For Hire" characters**: The London Broker Heath, Captain Unity, and The Great White North are each inconsistently classified as both "Corporate Structure" and "Independent Variable (For Hire)" across different roster passes — signals these characters are intentionally written as swing/mercenary pieces whose ultimate loyalty is unresolved and available for the writer to decide in Book 3.
- **Chapter 1 branching options proposed but left unresolved** (decision point for Book 3 opening): (1) Financial Showdown — Combs makes a financial move against Kennedy's authority; (2) Championship Decree — Kennedy and Combs covertly debate making Cain Elias the new World Champion; (3) Talent Loyalty Check — Cain Elias brutally tests a newly aligned AWE asset's loyalty. No selection was confirmed in the source text.
- **Akon "The Warrior" and Raja "The Ascendant"**: Both explicitly noted as potential late-arriving allies to the Alliance ("a potential last-minute, principled ally" / "could align with Marquis's freedom") — open recruitment threads for Book 3.
- **Shared finisher name collision**: "The Optimization Drive" (Wrist-clutch Exploder Suplex) is used as a finisher by both **Silas "The System"** and **Stick-Up** — likely an unintentional duplication in the source material worth flagging/resolving before porting into game data. "The Phantom Lock" is similarly shared between Kiko "The Ghost" Tanaka and Stick-Up.
